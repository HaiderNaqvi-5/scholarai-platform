# ScholarAI — Academic Research Contribution

> **Primary Research Areas:** Explainable Artificial Intelligence (XAI), Knowledge Graph-Augmented Recommendation Systems, AI Interview Simulation

---

## 1. Research Overview

ScholarAI is not merely an engineering application; it is designed to yield publishable research in applied machine learning and systems architecture. The platform addresses significant informational and trust asymmetries in the global higher education and scholarship ecosystem.

### Target Venues for Publication
- **Conferences:** ACM Recommender Systems (RecSys), AAAI Conference on Artificial Intelligence, Educational Data Mining (EDM).
- **Journals:** Expert Systems with Applications, IEEE Access, Education and Information Technologies.

---

## 2. Primary Research Contributions

The project claims novelty across three intersecting domains:

### Contribution 1: Explainable Scholarship Recommendations
Existing scholarship aggreators operate as simple keyword search engines. ScholarAI implements an XGBoost/Random Forest recommendation engine paired with **SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations)**.
- **Novelty:** Applying XAI to the domain of scholarship eligibility where recommendation transparency is currently unstudied.
- **Evaluation:** Quantitative (SHAP stability across retraining loops) and Qualitative (A/B testing user trust metrics with and without explanations).

### Contribution 2: Knowledge-Graph-Augmented Matching
Scholarships and student profiles inherently form a network (Student → Degree → Field ← Scholarship ← University ← Country).
- **Novelty:** Using a graph database (Neo4j) to inform recommendations, comparing traditional flat tabular models against graph-augmented recommendations.
- **Evaluation:** Measuring Precision@K, Recall@K, and NDCG against a baseline model.

### Contribution 3: Domain-Specific AI Interview Simulation
General-purpose LLMs lack the Rubric-based rigor required for strict scholarship interviews.
- **Novelty:** A multi-stage pipeline utilizing Whisper (Speech-to-Text) and GPT-4 evaluated against a strict multidimensional rubric (Confidence, Relevance, Clarity).
- **Evaluation:** Correlation studies comparing LLM-generated scores against human expert evaluators.

## 3. Dataset Strategy & Limitations

### The Data Scarcity Problem
> **Critical limitation:** There is no publicly available, large-scale dataset linking student profiles to exact scholarship outcomes (accept/reject).

### Mitigation Strategy:
1. **Initial Phase (Synthetic Data):** The model will be bootstrap-trained using procedurally generated synthetic student profiles combined with real scholarship requirements scraped from sources like DAAD and Fulbright. The heuristic labels will mimic basic logic (e.g., GPA > Min_GPA + Field Match).
2. **Production Phase (Feedback Loop):** The platform will collect voluntary user tracking data ("Did you win this scholarship?") to create a proprietary, fine-tunable dataset over time.

All research publications stemming from the initial system must explicitly disclose the use of synthetic baseline data.

---

## 4. Key Research Questions (RQs)

1. **RQ1:** To what extent do SHAP-based feature importance explanations increase student trust and actionable preparation compared to black-box recommendations?
2. **RQ2:** Does integrating a Neo4j Knowledge Graph improve the Recall@10 metric for scholarship discovery compared to a standard XGBoost tabular model?
3. **RQ3:** How accurately can a zero-shot LLM (GPT-4) simulate and score scholarship interview responses when compared to human evaluators, based on transcription generated by Whisper?

---

## 5. Security and Ethical Considerations

- **Algorithmic Bias:** Machine learning models trained on historical or synthetic data may inadvertently favor certain demographics or Western-centric educational markers. The research will include an equity audit across demographic cohorts.
- **Data Privacy:** Storing sensitive academic records requires GDPR compliance. The system mitigates data exposure by storing only anonymized profile data and never exposing personal identifiable information (PII) in model training.
